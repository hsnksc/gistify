## Macro Snapshot

### Employment and Fed Expectations

The May employment report shows the U.S. labor market remains resilient. Nonfarm payrolls rose by **172,000**, the unemployment rate held at **4.3%**, and average hourly earnings grew **3.4%** year-over-year. This picture suggests growth is not breaking down quickly, but wage inflation keeps the Fed cautious.

The next FOMC meeting is scheduled for **June 16–17, 2026**, and will release economic projections and the dot plot. The first policy signals of the new leadership term will be watched closely by markets.

**Market impact:** A pushback in rate-cut expectations could create volatility in technology and high-multiple stocks. At the same time, the jobs data supporting the growth side makes selective rebounds possible after sharp selloffs.

### Geopolitical Risk and Oil

Middle East headlines are keeping the energy risk premium intact. After mutual missile exchanges, Iran and Israel appear to have pulled back for now, but both sides are making conditional statements that leave room for new operations. Lebanon has become a harder red line for Iran.

**Market impact:** Brent crude holding above $94 supports the energy sector while keeping Fed risk alive through inflation expectations. Energy equities can serve as a limited portfolio hedge.

### AI Infrastructure Cycle

Major technology companies are expected to spend roughly **$650 billion** on AI infrastructure in 2026, with the bulk going to chips, servers, and data centers.

This theme provides medium-term support for NVDA, broad technology ETFs, and the data-center supply chain. As earnings season approaches, investors are likely to become more selective on guidance and earnings quality.
