## 4. Blackwell Launch ve Demand Pipeline

### 4.1 Blackwell Architecture (B100/B200)
- **Performance:** 20 PFLOPS FP4 = 10x H100 throughput. 192GB HBM3e. 4x perf/watt improvement.
- **Price:** $40,000-$50,000+ per unit (initial); premium over H100 ($25,000).
- **TDP:** 1000W = requires liquid cooling. Deployment complexity higher.
- **Status:** Full production, shipping since Q4 2025, ramping through 2026.

### 4.2 Supply Constraints (The Critical Bottleneck)
- **Lead Time:** 36-52 weeks for new Blackwell deployments (Mental-Momentum AI, Mayis 2026).
- **Bottleneck:** NOT raw silicon fabrication. It is **advanced packaging (CoWoS-L)** and **HBM memory** shortage.
- **TSMC CoWoS Capacity:** Doubled YoY from 35K wafers/month (2024) to 120K-130K wafers/month (late 2026). Still INSUFFICIENT.
- **Hyperscaler Lock-up:** Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon placed multi-billion-dollar forward orders in 2025, locking TSMC allocation through end of 2026.
- **Backlog:** 3.6 million units backlogged for B200 series.

### 4.3 Dual-Track Strategy: H200 Bridge
- NVIDIA maintains H200 production alongside Blackwell to bridge supply gap.
- H200 approved for China (10 firms including Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance) with 25% revenue-sharing fee.
- China orders: 2M+ H200 units for 2026 (exceeds 700K inventory). TSMC 4nm capacity re-negotiated.
- H200 revenue potential: $4B-$17B per quarter in 2026.

### 4.4 Revenue Pipeline
- **Data Center:** 90% of sales. 56% YoY increase in Q2 2026 (Blackwell-driven).
- **Gaming:** Record $16B in FY2026, up 41% YoY.
- **Automotive:** +30% YoY, self-driving partnerships expanding.
- **Networking:** $3.1B, up 20% YoY.
- **Total Revenue:** FY2026 guidance ~$78B+ (Q1 FY2027 consensus $78.41B).

### 4.5 Competitive Landscape
- **AMD:** MI300X/MI400 series gaining traction. MI450 H2 2026. OpenAI 6GW deal. But software gap (ROCm vs CUDA) remains significant. AMD = credible challenger, not yet threat.
- **Intel:** Braga chip delayed. Custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia) incremental but not displacing NVDA for large-scale workloads.
- **China Risk:** $50B China market effectively closed to U.S. industry. H200 exception = partial unlock, not full recovery. $17.1B annual GPU sales at risk.

> **Blackwell Verdict:** Supply constraint = pricing power remains with NVIDIA. Gross margins 73-75% sustainable. Demand > Supply for 4-6 quarters = revenue visibility extreme. This is a BULLISH structural setup.
