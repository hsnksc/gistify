01 Overview & Current Situation

NVIDIA has seen a pronounced price decline in recent months, but the pullback does not overshadow the stock's fundamental value and long-term potential. Shares fell from the all-time high of $236.54 set on May 14, 2026 to $196.93 — a correction of roughly 16.8%. The price is trading within its 52-week range of $158.39 – $236.54.

Core thesis: The decline stems from technical and macro pressure, not from any deterioration in fundamentals. A PEG ratio of 0.44 — well below 1 — signals the stock is trading below its value relative to its growth.

07 Risks

Market Share LossHigh

AMD, Intel, and hyperscalers' custom chips (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium) are gradually gaining market share.

Pricing PressureHigh

Rising competition and stronger customer bargaining power could push GPU prices lower.

China MarketHigh

Due to US export restrictions, NVIDIA is no longer present in the Chinese market — and that looks unlikely to change.

Supply ChainHigh

TSMC CoWoS capacity and HBM memory supply could constrain growth.

Demand SustainabilityMedium

The ROI on AI investments and the durability of demand remain uncertain; hyperscalers may hit borrowing limits.

Backstop ProgramMedium

Neocloud bankruptcy risk exists; NVIDIA's AA credit rating minimizes this exposure.

Vera CPU UncertaintyMedium

The CPU market is new territory for NVIDIA; success there will only become clear over time.

Liquidity & Balance SheetLow

Daily trading volume exceeds 120 million shares. A $38.5B buyback authorization and a growing dividend ($0.25/quarter) support the balance sheet. Data center concentration (88–90%) remains a diversification item to monitor.

08 Conclusion & Investment Call

NVIDIA is in an exceptionally strong position on fundamentals: the valuation is attractive, growth is rapid, margins are robust, and new revenue streams are being created. Despite short-term technical uncertainty, the mid- and long-term outlook is positive; the backstop program, offsetting the China loss, and the new product cycle keep the stock attractive for investors.

Investment Call: BUY — Scale in from the $190–195 zone in the short term; build the position on the growth story over the mid-to-long term.
Rationale
  1. Excellent fundamentals: Revenue growth 65–92%, margins 74%+, market share 75–90%
  2. Attractive valuation: Forward P/E ~20x, PEG 0.44 — cheap relative to growth
  3. Durable growth: Hyperscaler capex $4.7–5.3T (2025–2030), AI market $1T TAM (2030)
  4. Near-term catalysts: July earnings season, TSMC report, NVIDIA Q2 earnings (Aug 26)
  5. Long-term potential: $10–20T market cap, 5–7x revenue growth
