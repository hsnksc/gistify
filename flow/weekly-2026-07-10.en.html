🧭 Themes of the Week

▾
Theme 1 · Memory
Memory Supercycle Meets Its "Priced for Perfection" Test

SK hynix's $26.5-29.4B secondary ADS offering began trading on Nasdaq on July 10, just a week after Korean regulatory approval landed on July 3. Only three days earlier, Samsung reported the highest quarterly profit in its history — ₩89.4T (~$58.4B), up 1,810% YoY — yet the stock fell 6.4-10% the same day on a slight revenue miss, which the source report frames as a classic "priced for perfection" crowded-trade unwind. Samsung + SK Hynix now make up 55.3% of the KOSPI (up from 36.1% at end-2025); BofA's May survey found 73% of fund managers call "long global semis" the most crowded trade on record. Meanwhile Micron remains the sector's value outlier — PEG 0.04-0.14x despite the smallest HBM share (15-20%) of the big three. Bottom line: HBM supply is sold out through 2026-27 and DRAM/NAND prices are still rising double digits QoQ — the demand side is intact, but Korean margin debt sits at record highs and leveraged ETFs now make up 31-38% of daily volume.

Theme 2 · Macro
AI CapEx Is Now Overtaking the Defense Budget

Goldman Sachs models $7.6T in cumulative AI infrastructure capital deployment for 2026-2031; a separate deep-research piece finds combined US AI infrastructure capex will hit 3.0-3.3% of GDP in 2027, crossing defense spending's ~2.7% for the first time in history — a finding described as "robust" across CBO/IMF/Fed/even the Trump OMB's own assumptions. Meta/Microsoft/Amazon/Alphabet's combined 2025-2030 capex was just revised up $800B to $5.3T. The most-repeated caution across sources: no independent source — Moody's, JPMorgan, McKinsey, Gartner, MIT — has concrete GPU-cluster utilization data, making the overbuild-vs-capacity-constrained debate "technically unresolvable" with current disclosure. Financing math is tightening too: JPMorgan estimates a $1.4T gap between 2026-2030 financing needs and available sources; Oracle's 5-year CDS has tripled since September 2025. The NVIDIA→CoreWeave/OpenAI circular-financing loop keeps getting compared to the 2000 Lucent/Nortel/Cisco vendor-financing collapse.

Theme 3 · NVDA
NVIDIA's "Dominance Discount" Is Being Re-Rated

NVDA's forward P/E compressed this week, across sources, from a 5-year average near 72x to roughly 16-22x, with PEG down to 0.44-0.60x — one of the cheapest large-caps in the market alongside Broadcom. The bull case rests on institutional flows: $243.0B in net 12-month inflows, 4,567 buying institutions vs. 3,602 selling — framed explicitly as "retail panic vs. institutional accumulation." The bear case is share erosion, not demand collapse: NVIDIA's CoWoS packaging allocation is projected to fall from ~60% today to 55% by 2027F as AMD and Broadcom-designed ASICs (Google TPU, Meta MTIA, Microsoft Maia) gain share, with JPMorgan projecting NVIDIA's inference share could fall toward ~40% by 2028. Vera Rubin is the swing catalyst: the platform's CPO-driven network content per rack jumps roughly 29x versus the current GB300 generation ($315K → $9.4M) — either NVIDIA's next monetization leg or its next execution risk, depending on ramp timing. Next checkpoint: Q2 FY2027 earnings on August 26, 2026.

Theme 4 · Photonics
Photonics/CPO: The Bottleneck After Memory

Goldman Sachs models the optical-networking TAM growing from $18.2B (2026E) to $154.3B by 2028 — roughly 9x — as copper interconnects hit their 2-meter physical limit; Co-Packaged Optics' share of that pie rises from ~6% to ~58-59%. Supply is the real constraint: China controls ~70% of global indium production, and February 2025 export-licensing requirements spiked InP wafer prices roughly 250% ($1,400 → $5,000); Lumentum says it is "sold out through end of 2027" on CPO-related lines. NVIDIA has already locked in $4B of direct equity/purchase commitments across Coherent and Lumentum to secure laser supply; Broadcom, Intel and Cisco together hold roughly 50.6% of the silicon-photonics market that earlier, less-rigorous coverage overlooked entirely. A historical caution flagged directly in the source material: the 2021-22 optical correction cost Lumentum 40%+ of revenue and took 43 months to recover; today's optical names trade at multiples explicitly compared to the dot-com era (COHR ~181x, LITE 141-180x trailing P/E). Worth keeping: "Photonics is probably the next bottleneck after memory, but these stocks have run a lot and can't go up in a straight line." — Matthew Tuttle, FOTO ETF Manager.
