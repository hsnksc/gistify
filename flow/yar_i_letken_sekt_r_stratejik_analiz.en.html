🧠 Second- and Third-Order Strategic Implications

▾

Production Bottleneck and the Balance of Power

Value creation is shifting from pure design to physical manufacturing capacity. The gulf between demand for ARM's new AGI CPU architecture and its available production capacity is proof that power is moving toward foundry and metrology tools (ONTO, ENTG).

⚖️ ARM AGI CPU: Demand vs. Production Capacity ($ Billion)
Demand is exactly 20x secured production capacity — the clearest evidence yet that power is shifting from chip design toward foundry and metrology tools.

Hardware-Software Convergence

ONTO's Rigaku acquisition and LSCC's AMI move show these companies shifting from a "cyclical sales" model to a "sticky software" model that captures a share of data-center budgets.

HIGH-MARGIN STRATEGY
The RISC-V Structural Threat: ARM launching its own branded CPUs has turned giants like Apple and Qualcomm into rivals. This is likely to aggressively accelerate investment in the open-source RISC-V architecture. ⚠️ ARM's royalty engine is at risk.
