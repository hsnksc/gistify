📉 Semiconductor Cycle & "Multiple Compression" Analysis

▾

The global semiconductor sector faces an intense cyclical-correction risk, triggered by monetary expansion and commodity rotation (M2 money supply reaching $23 trillion). The sharp reversal in liquidity-sensitive assets like silver from their peaks has raised cycle-top fears in chips as well.

This has produced deep multiple compression in NVIDIA shares. Even though the company's trailing-twelve-month revenue grew 71%, its trailing P/E fell from a 5-year historical average of 69.62x to 32.31x. Per Morgan Stanley and BofA, the forward P/E multiple compressing to roughly 18x signals the market is pricing in a sharp slowdown in AI investment appetite.

Meanwhile, Big Tech's AI capital expenditure is on track to exceed $800 billion in 2026 and approach $1.1 trillion in 2027 — nearing the US defense budget. That backdrop explains the polarization between the stock's enormous short interest ($16.7 billion) and retail sentiment (91.8% long).

💰 Rack-Level BOM Inflation: Blackwell vs. Vera Rubin

▾

As NVIDIA's sales mix shifts to rack-scale systems (NVL72), the supply chain is seeing unprecedented cost inflation. Per Morgan Stanley, the Blackwell NVL72 (GB300) rack cost of roughly $4,000,000 rises 95% to $7,800,000 for the Vera Rubin VR200 (NVL72) rack. The main driver is a 435% jump in HBM4 and LPDDR5X memory costs.

ASIC CompetitionCustom AI Silicon Threat

To break NVIDIA's market dominance, large language model developers are building custom inference-focused chips (ASICs). The latest example: Chinese AI champion DeepSeek has launched an effort to design its own inference chip tailored to its models.

DeepSeek is targeting vertical integration with $7.4 billion raised from Tencent, CATL, and China's national fund. As AI operating costs shift from training to inference, cheap, low-power ASICs become attractive. Similar trends are visible at OpenAI (the "Jalapeno" chip), Anthropic (Samsung partnership), and Google (the TPU line).

NVIDIA's Defense: ASIC development requires billions in budget and a minimum 3–5 year design cycle; Chinese firms are cut off from TSMC foundries and HBM memory supply by sanctions; NVIDIA's $119 billion in advance supply commitments corner global production capacity.

Silicon PhotonicsCPO Bottleneck & Marvell Partnership

As AI systems scale out across data centers, the biggest bottleneck is moving electrical signals over data cabling (bandwidth and power loss). Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) offers the one structural fix.

Morgan Stanley's CPO supply-chain research finds the market's expectation of 100,000 CPO units shipped in 2026 is overly optimistic — real shipments will likely land around ~23,000 units. The core constraint is TSMC's photonic integrated circuit (PIC) capacity, just 500 units/month in 2025.

To get past this optical bottleneck, NVIDIA made a $2 billion convertible preferred investment in Marvell Technology on March 31, 2026, and established the "NVLink Fusion" ecosystem, folding Marvell's Celestial AI (Photonic Fabric) patents into its own architecture; similar investments went to Coherent and Lumentum.
