📌 Strategic Assessment

As AI model parameter sizes grow exponentially, ultra-low-latency cluster architecture has become critical. Per Goldman Sachs analysts, compute gains are now determined not only at the chip level but by data-transfer speed and rack-to-rack interconnect efficiency. As traditional copper reaches its physical limit (a 2-meter reach ceiling), Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) and standard Silicon Photonics (SiPh) are going mainstream at the board level.

Scale-up Dominance

69% of the $154B TAM (~$106B) will come from tightly-coupled vertical-scaling (scale-up) networks.

CPO Transition Pace

2026 is the official commercial start of the CPO transition; 29% penetration in scale-out switches is expected by 2028.

Cost Leadership

At 1.6T, the SiPh architecture delivers a 32% net BOM advantage and +7pp gross margin over EML laser solutions.

⚙️ CPO Architecture Mechanism

In traditional optical modules, after leaving the switch chip (ASIC), electrical signals travel 15–30 cm across the board to reach the module cage. This causes electrical losses and requires power-hungry DSPs (retimers). CPO places the optical engine right next to the ASIC, shrinking that path to millimeters.

  • Lower Power: Per-link electrical draw drops from 30W to 9W (~70% saving).
  • DSP-free Design: The millimetric physical link removes the need for a DSP retimer.
  • Higher Density: Faceplate cage areas free up, enabling direct fiber egress.
Engineering Bottleneck: Because integration is high at the board level, a single failed optical engine makes servicing the whole board difficult. Lasers (ELS) are therefore hosted externally on the front panel as hot-swappable units.

🌋 Indium Phosphide (InP) Crisis Analysis

Silicon Photonics waveguides depend at the raw-material level on Indium Phosphide (InP) substrate to carry high-speed light. China holds ~70% of global indium production, giving it a monopoly over a critical supply chain.

Feb 2025 — Export Controls: China's Ministry of Commerce imposed a license requirement on InP exports; substrate prices jumped from $1,400 to $5,000 per wafer.
Capacity Bottleneck: AXT Inc (the world's 2nd-largest InP producer) only received its first licenses in June 2025; backlog at buyers like Lumentum stretched into 2028.
Partial Easing — May 2026: China partially relaxed InP shipment permits, easing the global transceiver bottleneck; still, Chinese raw-material giants are ramping capacity fast.
