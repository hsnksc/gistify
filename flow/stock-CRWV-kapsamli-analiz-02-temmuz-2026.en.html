The Crunch of the Core Scientific (CORZ) Merger Myth

The information often cited in the market is completely false. The $9 billion share swap deal failed to receive the required votes at Core Scientific's special shareholder meeting and Officially canceled on October 30, 2025 (SEC Form 8-K).

590 MW HPC Contract Continues: Even though the merger was cancelled, 590 MW HPC contract is still in force; in 12 years $10.2B revenue potential carries. Core Scientific is obliged to deliver a further 280 MW by the end of 2026.
Delay Scandal & Class-Action: In November 2025, CoreWeave dropped its guidance. The WSJ revealed on December 15, 2025 that the delays had been known to builder Core Scientific since February. "Generational Silo" Logic: According to Wells Fargo and AccruedInt analysis; Meta will likely introvert the latest Rubin/GB300 clusters, selling out the older H100/H200 capacity. This is not "oversupply", "generational silo" is the logic. Each generation of GPU creates its own optimization and customer base.

Considering the long-term debt of $17.3 billion, a possible price war could make the financing model unsustainable.
