03 The Architecture Behind China's Cost Advantage

The primary architectural innovation enabling China's cost advantage is Mixture of Experts (MoE), which dramatically reduces computational requirements for both training and inference — maintaining large total parameter counts for capability while activating only a small subset per inference.

🧠 Parameter Activation Ratio by Model

A lower activation ratio means 3–5x lower compute cost versus dense transformer models. DeepSeek V4-Pro leads with the lowest ratio at 3.1%.

Beyond MoE, complementary innovations include sparse attention mechanisms, multi-head latent attention (memory compression), multi-token prediction, and DeepSeek's DSpark speculative decoding framework (60–85% faster generation without quality loss).

Training Cost: DeepSeek R1 was trained for roughly $6M (671B parameters, 37B activated) versus GPT-4's estimated $100M+ training cost. Per a RAND report, Chinese models generally cost 1/6 to 1/4 of comparable US systems.

Scale economies (a projected 25x increase in daily token consumption from 2026–2030) and architectural efficiency drive both model tiers to positive EBIT margin by 2030.

Drivers of margin expansion: scale economies (35T to 460T daily tokens), continued MoE efficiency gains, a shift from pure open-source to revenue-sharing open-weight licenses, and channel diversification into international and enterprise segments.

The Open-Source Monetization Challenge: Open-source models can be hosted by third parties (e.g. Alibaba Cloud's Bailian MaaS) without direct payment to the model creator. Solution path: shift from MIT license to open-weight + community license with revenue-sharing agreements for commercial use — a model pioneered by MiniMax's M series.

05 Market Dynamics: The OpenRouter Evidence

OpenRouter, the largest platform routing requests across hundreds of AI models, provides the clearest evidence of China's rising share. In the week of Feb 9–15, 2026, Chinese models passed US models for the first time (4.12T vs 2.94T tokens). US models held ~70% share in mid-2025; by June 2026 that fell to ~30%.

🇨🇳 China Token Usage Share by Task Type

Chinese models command 85%+ share in coding and agentic tasks, well above parity — the gap is narrower in general and data tasks.

Programming workloads grew from ~11% to over 50% of total OpenRouter token volume through 2025; agentic workflows now account for 50%+ of all output tokens. A single overnight coding run can invoke a model thousands of times, making per-million-token pricing the dominant cost factor.

Why China Wins in Coding/Agents: (1) Cost Sensitivity — each task can burn hundreds of thousands of tokens, making a 6–8x price gap existential. (2) Performance Parity — GLM-5.2 and Qwen 3.7 Max match coding performance. (3) Open-Weight Availability — enables self-hosting and customization. (4) Community Adoption — developer communities have rapidly embraced Chinese models for price/performance.
From Token Maximization to ROI Priority: In late 2025–early 2026, enterprises equated high token consumption with productivity. From 2026 on, clear task boundaries, measurable outputs, and actual business impact take priority — a shift that favors Chinese models' better economics on high-volume, long-duration tasks.

10 Conclusion: The New Economics of AI

The LLM market is shifting from a capability race to cost competition, with China as the clear leader in the commodity segment. Through architectural innovations like MoE, Chinese providers achieved a structural cost advantage enabling aggressive pricing while maintaining performance parity with US models.
