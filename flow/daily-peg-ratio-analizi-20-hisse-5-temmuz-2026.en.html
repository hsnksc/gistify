💡 Cross-Dimensional Insights

▾
1. Memory Supercycle - Historic Opportunity: MU (0.04x) and SNDK (0.03x) are not just "cheap" - AI data center demand may have permanently raised the earnings base of the memory industry. TrendForce raised its 2026 storage forecast by 61% to $889B. Forward PEG around 0.05 - the cheapest large-cap stocks in the market.
2. META - Most Mispriced Mega-Cap: 0.86x PEG, $1.48T market cap, $2.10 dividend and 32.8% margin make META the clearest "buy" signal in the S&P 500 on pure PEG basis. The user mispriced it by 43%.
3. AI Capex Dark Period: Big Tech's $650B+ AI spending is pressuring current FCF and making P/E multiples look inflated. But revenue returns are delayed 12-24 months. GOOGL and AMZN may look "expensive" now but could be the cheapest in 2 years.
4. PEG Can Vary 2-4x for the Same Stock: NOW ranges from 1.03x to 4.43x; META ranges from 0.89x to 3.23x. Without specifying which P/E (trailing/forward) and which growth rate (1Y/3Y/5Y) is used, PEG comparison is comparing apples to oranges.
5. HIMS - PEG's Most Dangerous Blind Spot: The user said 0.9x, actual is 9.66x+. 10x too cheap estimate. Confusing revenue growth with earnings growth actively makes PEG misleading.
6. INTC and SOFI - PEG Invalid: PEG is mathematically meaningless for companies with negative TTM earnings or negative earnings growth. For these companies, EV/Revenue, Price/Book, or SOTP valuation should be used.
