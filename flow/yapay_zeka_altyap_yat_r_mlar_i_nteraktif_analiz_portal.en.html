💵 The Core Financial Problem in AI Investment

▾

Big Tech and hyperscaler AI capital expenditure has reached an annualized $750 billion run rate as of 2026. The industry's common "$1.5 trillion question" framing tries to justify this spending through linear payback logic, but the real investor question should be whether return on capital clears the weighted average cost of capital (WACC).

At the micro level, building a top-tier "frontier facility" AI data center at 1 GW scale costs roughly $50.5 billion. Under a financial-equivalence model targeting a 10% WACC and a 50% pre-D&A operating margin, that facility must generate at least $22 billion in annual revenue to deliver its investors the return they were promised.

🐂 The Bull Case

The path where trillion-dollar investments clear their financial cost of capital

1. Agentic & Reasoning Demand: As reasoning-based models mature, token consumption per transaction rises, multiplying revenue volume even as unit price falls.
2. Inference Dominance: Off-the-shelf inference-traffic revenue overtakes costly training spend, expanding margins.
3. Physical Infrastructure Flexibility: 20-year power/EPC/shell investments successfully host 2–3 shifting chip generations.

🐻 The Bear Case

Write-down / "capital burn cycle" risks in infrastructure investment

1. Scarcity-Pricing Normalization: Large-scale chip supply hitting the market collapses spot GPU rental prices.
2. Rapid Chip Obsolescence: The pace of tech generations shortens hardware economic life below 5.5 years, increasing depreciation pressure.
3. Cohort Stacking: $750B in new infrastructure is added every year while global GenAI revenue growth fails to keep pace with a 74% annual threshold.
