🧪 Concepts: Short Float, DTC and Squeeze Dynamics

Short Float & Days-to-Cover

Short Float (%): the percentage of shares sold short relative to the freely tradable float.

Short Float % = (Shares Short / Free Float) × 100

Days-to-Cover (DTC): the theoretical number of days short sellers would need to close their positions.

DTC = Shares Short / Average Daily Volume
Short Squeeze & FTD

Short Squeeze: a loop where a rapid price rise forces short sellers to buy in a panic, which pushes the price up exponentially.

Fail-to-Deliver (FTD): the short party's failure to deliver shares on settlement day. Accumulated FTDs can trigger mandatory buy-ins — a critical hidden catalyst behind parabolic squeeze events.

Short float ratios of 20% and above mark the critical threshold for the risk class.
