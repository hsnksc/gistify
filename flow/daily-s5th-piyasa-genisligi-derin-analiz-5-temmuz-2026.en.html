📐 What Is S5TH? Definition and Calculation

▾

S5TH (S&P 500 Stocks Above 200-Day Moving Average) is the percentage of S&P 500 stocks that close above their own 200-day simple moving average (SMA).

Formula: S5TH = (Number of stocks closing above 200-GMA / 500) × 100

Technical Details

  • Calculation: Strictly uses SMA (Simple Moving Average). The EMA claim (WallStreetMojo) conflicts with all primary sources.
  • Update: End-of-day (EOD). No intraday real-time version is available.
  • Membership changes: S&P 500 is calculated based on the current 500 constituents. Announcements are at 17:15 ET, implementation occurs 1-5 days later.
  • Timeframe: 200 trading days ≈ 9.5 months (200 ÷ 21 trading days).
  • Data range: Data available since 1980 on Barchart and EODData.

⚠️ Limitations and Risks

▾

Known Limitations of S5TH

  • Lagged Signal: Because it uses the 200-GMA, it lags trend reversals by weeks/months. During 2007-2009 it produced 4 whipsaws at the 50% threshold.
  • EOD Calculation: It is end-of-day data; it misses intraday moves and sudden breakdowns.
  • Cap-Weighted Impact: S5TH does not directly reflect S&P 500 cap-weighted (SPY) behavior. It is closer to RSP (equal-weight) internals.
  • Membership Changes: Large newly added stocks to the S&P 500 (e.g., Tesla in December 2020) can create anomalous effects.
  • Sector Bias: When the technology sector (XLK) narrows, S5TH generally falls, and the strength of other sectors may be masked.
🚨 S5TH Should Not Be Used Alone: Schwab officially warns that "breadth can be misleading, not lean on it as their only signal." StockCharts states it is "not immune to whipsaws." S5TH should always be evaluated alongside the McClellan Oscillator, VIX, AD Line, and price action.

Phantom Rally Detector

One of the most valuable uses of S5TH is to monitor the gap between SPY (cap-weighted) and S5TH:

  • SPY high, S5TH low = Narrow leadership (Mag7 carrying, broad market weak) = Phantom rally risk
  • SPY high, S5TH high = Broad-based healthy trend
  • SPY low, S5TH high = Rare; broad market showing resilience, cap-weight collapsing
  • SPY low, S5TH low = Broad-based selling = Real bear market

In 2026, the S&P 500 is near ATH (~7,500) but S5TH is only around 60% (below the historical bull average of 73%). This is a narrow leadership / fragility signal.

S5TH and Recession Relationship

S5TH < 20% is NOT a recession predictor. It is a market bottom indicator. The year 2022 is the clearest proof of this distinction:

💡 Inference: S5TH < 20% → time for "bottom fishing," NOT time for "recession avoidance." S5TH should not be used alone as a macro decision tool. It should be used TOGETHER with macro indicators such as yield curve, ISM, and unemployment.

FED Lag Effect

FED rate hike/cut cycles affect S5TH with a 3-6 month lag. This confirms that S5TH is not a "real-time" but a "coincident-to-lagging" indicator:

  • S5TH starts to decline 3-6 months after FED rate hikes begin
  • In 2008, S5TH collapsed despite rate cuts having already started
  • In 2022-2023, S5TH remained above 20% despite aggressive hikes

Application: Do not look at S5TH immediately after an FED meeting; wait 3-6 months. FED decisions can give position-entry signals BEFORE S5TH.
