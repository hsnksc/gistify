🚀 Bull Case Thesis — 3 Key Arguments

▾
1. Demand Overwhelmingly Exceeds Supply — And This Is Structural
NVIDIA CEO: $1T+ backlog. TSMC fully booked (through mid-2027). HBM sold out through end of 2026. Of 12 GW of US data center plans, 7 GW were canceled. Gartner: 40% of AI data centers will be power-constrained by 2027. This is not a cyclical shortage — building infrastructure takes 3–7 years. Neoclouds are companies that already have contracted power.
2. Meta Compute Sale Validates Demand, Not Competition
Meta can only sell first-party bare-metal capacity. The $12B Vera Rubin from Nebius and $35.2B capacity from CoreWeave cannot be resold externally. Meta's cloud is a different tier: older GPUs, no enterprise SLA, different customer base. Wells Fargo: "Meta compute bullish for CRWV." GPU prices rising QoQ — proof that demand is strong.
3. Backlog Value Not Yet Priced In
CRWV $99.4B backlog / ~$46B market cap = 2.2x. Contracts are take-or-pay, 5–6 year terms, through 2031–2032. Meta ($35.2B) + OpenAI ($22.4B) are the largest customers. NBIS: Meta $12B + Microsoft $17.4B. Morgan Stanley: CoreWeave = "certainty", Nebius = "potential". CRWV at 8 GW in 2030 = $80–160B annual revenue capacity. Currently trading at 0.7x 2030 sales.
