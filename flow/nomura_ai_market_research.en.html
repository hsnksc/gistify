🏭 1. TSMC CoWoS Capacity & Facts

▾

Current (2026) capacity sits around 1.1 million wafers and is fully reserved. NVIDIA holds ~60% of capacity (~595,000 wafers). NVIDIA, Broadcom, and AMD together make up >85% of total capacity.

📈 2027 CoWoS Capacity Outlook (Million Wafers)
The 200,000-wafer gap between TSMC's 2.0M target and Nomura's 1.8M estimate will keep market supply tight.
Primary Bottleneck Shift
As CoW (Chip-on-Wafer) issues resolve, the WoS (Wafer-on-Substrate) process emerges as a bigger bottleneck from H2 2026, spilling into 2027.
2027 Projection
TSMC targets 2.0M wafers (+80% YoY); Nomura expects 1.8M wafers (+60-65% YoY).
Future Roadmap
14x reticle-size CoWoS planned for 2027; SoIC and CoPoS volume production planned for 2028.
Competitive Threat: Google's TPU v9 project is partnering with MediaTek to use Intel's EMIB-T alternative packaging technology. TSMC is accelerating SoIC development in response.

AMD: Rack-Scale Systems
MI455X GPU unveiled at CES 2026. Helios platform with independent cold plates and flexible-hose distributed cooling (>1,200W power density). OpenAI, Meta, and Anthropic are primary customers.
