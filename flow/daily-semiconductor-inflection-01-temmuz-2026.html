01 Executive Summary

▾

Artificial intelligence is precipitating a profound rewrite of the semiconductor manufacturing industry's underlying logic. For decades, the primary vector for performance gains was the geometric scaling of the transistor — Moore's Law and Dennard scaling. However, at sub-3nm nodes, the traditional levers of miniaturization are fracturing under physical, thermal, and economic constraints.

The new growth model relies on three pillars: 3D transistor architectures (GAA to cFET), backside power delivery networks (BSPDN), and aggressive materials substitution across the manufacturing stack. This structural pivot will trigger a systemic revaluation of materials, equipment, specialty chemicals, and advanced packaging.

Inflection: 2026 is the critical ramp and pilot phase; 2027 is the inflection point for high-volume mass production. From 2028–2030, full adoption establishes a new equilibrium that redistributes profit pools toward advanced materials and packaging ecosystems.

Yapay zeka, yarı iletken üretim endüstrisinin temel mantığını derinden yeniden yazıyor. Onlarca yıldır performans kazanımlarının birincil vektörü, transistörün geometrik ölçeklendirmesiydi — Moore Yasası ve Dennard ölçeklendirmesi. Ancak 3nm altı düğümlerde, geleneksel miniaturizasyon kolları fiziksel, termal ve ekonomik kısıtlar altında kırılıyor.

Yeni büyüme modeli üç temel üzerine kurulu: 3D transistör mimarileri (GAA'dan cFET'e), arkadan güç iletimi ağları (BSPDN) ve üretim yığınında agresif malzeme değişimi. Bu yapısal dönüşüm, malzemeler, ekipman, özel kimyasallar ve gelişmiş paketleme sektöründe sistemik bir yeniden değerlemeyi tetikleyecek.

02 Post-Moore: The Scaling Challenge

▾

Below the 3nm threshold, sub-threshold leakage, parasitic capacitance, thermal dissipation, and metal pitch compression converge to create a formidable barrier. Metal pitch — the minimum center-to-center distance between adjacent interconnect lines — serves as the primary metric for logic density.

Without further optical innovations, the scaling curve threatens to flatline at ~16 nm. To push pitch to ~12 nm for angstrom-class nodes, the industry must transition to High-NA EUV lithography. Concurrently, transistor architectures must evolve vertically: GAA nanosheets, and subsequently stacked cFET structures, enable higher drive currents and superior electrostatic control.

04 Photolithography Paradigm Shift

▾

As the industry pushes toward the angstrom era, photolithography requires a decisive transition to High-NA EUV systems. These tools, priced at approximately $400 million per unit, increase the numerical aperture from 0.33 to 0.55.

The Rayleigh criterion dictates resolution: R = k₁ · λ / NA. A higher NA improves critical dimension (R), but dramatically reduces the depth of focus (DOF), which scales inversely with the square of the NA. This strictly requires ultra-thin photoresist layers.

This privatization shields JSR from short-term market pressures, allowing a ruthless "Selection and Concentration" strategy. 06 Advanced Packaging & Hybrid Bonding

▾

As monolithic die scaling yields diminishing returns, the industry has embraced chiplet architectures and heterogeneous integration. The ultimate bottleneck is interconnect density and bandwidth between chiplets, particularly for AI accelerators paired with HBM stacks.

SoIC hybrid bonding eliminates physical microbumps entirely, utilizing direct Cu-to-Cu interconnects embedded within a dielectric layer (SiO₂ or SiCN). This increases interconnect density by a full order of magnitude, propelling inter-die bandwidth from ~200 GB/s to 1 TB/s and beyond. SoIC has been in volume production since 2022, with demand surging in 2026–2027 driven by HBM4 and AI chiplet cycles.

For decades, ABF organic substrates served as the industry standard. Glass-core substrates offer a tunable CTE precisely matched to silicon, dramatically reducing thermal stress and eliminating warpage in large-format packaging.

08 Backside Power Delivery (BSPDN)

▾

Traditionally, both signal routing interconnects and the power delivery network shared the front side of the silicon wafer. As transistor density increases, this shared architecture leads to severe routing congestion and significant voltage drop (IR drop) over microscopic interconnect paths, starving transistors of power.

By separating the power delivery network and routing it through wider, lower-resistance metal lines on the backside of the wafer, signal integrity on the front side is preserved and power efficiency is dramatically enhanced. This requires highly complex new manufacturing steps: extreme wafer thinning and carrier wafer bonding.

Geleneksel olarak, hem sinyal yönlendirme bağlantı hatları hem de güç iletim ağı silikon wafer'ın ön yüzünü paylaşırdı. Transistör yoğunluğu arttıkça, bu paylaşılan mimari ciddi yönlendirme tıkanıklığına ve önemli voltaj düşümüne (IR düşümü) yol açar.

Güç iletim ağını ayırarak ve wafer'ın arka yüzü üzerinden daha geniş, daha düşük dirençli metal hatlarla yönlendirerek, ön yüzdeki sinyal bütünlüğü korunur ve güç verimliliği dramatik şekilde artırılır.

11 TSMC, Geopolitics & Localization

▾

TSMC acts as the primary amplifier of supply chain shocks. 2026 capex guidance is $52–56 billion (not $70B in 2027 as some reports claimed). This supports expansion of advanced wafer fabrication and packaging facilities globally, spanning Taiwan, US, Japan, and Germany.

12 Strategic Investment Implications

▾

The evidence points toward the specialized materials and advanced substrate processing layers as the most mispriced opportunity. Front-end equipment providers and pure-play foundry equities have largely absorbed AI premiums. The underlying atomic and chemical enablers remain widely underappreciated.

Vector 1
Substrate & TGV Innovators
As organic ABF substrates hit thermal/electrical limits, the pivot to glass is unavoidable. LPKF (LIDE), E&R Engineering, Ingentec — represent the absolute structural bottleneck for next-gen advanced packaging. Essential for 2027, yet largely outside macro-semiconductor trades.
Vector 2
Regional Specialty Chemicals
TSMC's capex is known. What is underappreciated is the localized velocity of capital re-routing into regional supply chains. Small-cap Taiwanese suppliers (AEMC, Sunlit, IV Technologies) capturing share from entrenched Japanese incumbents.

Conclusion: The timeline is rigid and defined by the physics of semiconductor scaling. 2026 is the volatile ramp setup phase; 2027 is the inflection point for mass production; 2028–2030 cements new market leaders. The most robust capitalization on the AI computing trade requires moving one layer deeper into the supply chain stack — anchoring positions in the specialized materials, localized consumables, and novel substrate technologies that make the angstrom era physically possible.

Kanıtlar, uzmanlaşmış malzemeler ve gelişmiş substrat işleme katmanlarına en çok yanlış fiyatlanmış fırsat olarak işaret ediyor. Ön uç ekipman sağlayıcıları ve saf foundry hisseleri büyük ölçüde AI primlerini içeriyor.

Vektör 1
Substrat ve TGV İnovatörleri
Organik ABF substratlar termal/elektriksel sınırlara ulaştıkça, cam geçişi kaçınılmazdır. LPKF (LIDE), E&R Engineering, Ingentec — yeni nesil gelişmiş paketleme için mutlak yapısal darboğaz. 2027 için zorunlu, ancak tipik makro-yarı iletken ticaretlerinin dışında.
Vektör 2
