Warsh's first meeting was more hawkish than the market expected. The dot plot's median year-end rate forecast for 2026 rose from 3.4% to 3.8%, implying zero rate cuts in 2026 (or even potential for one hike). On June 19, the market repriced hawkishly: tech stocks sold off sharply (Infosys -6.75%, TCS -3.55%), gold -1.70%, and 10Y yields rose.
