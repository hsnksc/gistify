⚙️ 1. What is a Leveraged ETF? How Does It Work?

▾

A Leveraged ETF (LETF) is a structured product that promises to magnify the daily return of the underlying asset by 2× or 3×. This expectation is valid only for a single-day period; multi-day holding creates compounding returns that deviate beyond the theoretical multiplier.

📊 Case: 2022 Bear Market — QQQ -32.49%, TQQQ -79.08%

Nasdaq-100 (QQQ) fell 32.49% from its November 2021 peak to its December 2022 trough. TQQQ lost 79.08% in the same period — below the theoretical 3× multiplication of 97.47%, but far above the simple 3× expectation of 65.7%. The 48-point gap is the cost of path dependency: the grinding bear market spread over 200+ trading days, accumulating each daily reset compounding asymmetry, pushed the loss to 79.08%. TQQQ needed a 486% gain to recover from the trough; it took 486 trading days (approximately 23 months).

For leveraged ETFs and volatility products, collapses are not merely portfolio losses but historical events that expose structural design flaws and market fragilities.

March 2020 — COVID Crash
29 leveraged/inverse ETF/ETNs experienced closure or automatic acceleration. When the S&P 500 fell 34% in 3 weeks, 3x products entered a structural liquidation cascade via daily reset.
2022 — TQQQ / UPRO Drawdown
TQQQ peak-to-trough -81.7%; UPRO -60%+. QQQ only -32.7%, S&P 500 -19.5%. The grinding bear market's daily reset penalty.
September 2024 — JPMorgan $15B Rebalancing
Leveraged/inverse ETFs sold ~$15 billion in stocks in a single day; Nasdaq-100 fell 3%. Nomura: S&P 500 futures dropped 34 points in 17 minutes (to the 5,516 level).
Index vs 3x ETF Drawdown: S&P 500 -19% → UPRO -60%+, Nasdaq-100 -33% → TQQQ -81.7%
🔴 Feedback Loop: Short VIX ETPs were forced to buy VIX futures for end-of-day rebalancing. These purchases pushed futures prices higher, and higher futures forced even more buying — a classic feedback loop. At 16:08, 115,862 contracts (~25% of the market) traded in a single minute. Dealers selling VIX futures hedged by shorting E-mini S&P 500 futures; creating additional pressure on the stock market.

💡 TQQQ Option Premium: TQQQ option premium ≈ 4.9x QQQ option. 3x ETF leverage + option delta/gamma = double leverage on an already leveraged product. The Montreal Exchange (2019) study confirmed linear IV scaling; but in OTM options, the vega profile deviates from linearity and gamma risk becomes explosive.
🔴 0DTE Gamma Seller Risk: TQQQ's daily rebalancing move is in the same direction as the market (structural negative gamma). During the transition from 0DTE gamma "pinning" regime to "breakout" regime, the dealer hedge cycle shifts from stabilization to amplification. The 15:30-16:00 rebalancing accumulation structurally inflates 0DTE option premiums — and then rapidly erodes them via physical delivery/expire worthless outcome.
⚠️ SQQQ Put Writing Trap: SQQQ can rally 100%+ in a bear market (inverse ETF's bull market rally). Put sellers are exposed to this asymmetric tail. TQQQ call writing similarly, in sudden bear market rallies, can take the call seller to margin call.
