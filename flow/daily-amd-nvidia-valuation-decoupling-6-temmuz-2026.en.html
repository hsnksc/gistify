🔬 7 Cross-Dimension Insights

▾
Insight 1: Narrative Premium, Not Growth Premium
AMD's 2.6x Fwd P/E premium cannot be explained by growth rates (AMD ~46% vs NVDA ~85-120%) or margins (AMD 13% vs NVDA 63%). This is an "AI underdog" story premium. In the DCF base scenario, AMD is $490 (current $518), NVIDIA $320 (current $195). The market prices AMD for perfection and NVIDIA for slowdown.
Insight 2: Custom Silicon > AMD as Real NVIDIA Threat
The biggest threat to NVIDIA is not AMD, but custom silicon (TPU, Trainium, MTIA, Maia). $25B+ market in 2026, share 20.9%→27.8%. Hyperscalers are reducing NVIDIA dependency while also narrowing AMD's open-market opportunity. Broadcom has set a $100B custom AI chip revenue target for 2027.
Insight 3: "Stock-for-Watts" Model — A New Risk
Meta and OpenAI each received 160M share warrants (total 320M shares, ~20% potential dilution). AMD is giving shares to customers to become customers. Morgan Stanley: "financial commitment flows from AMD to customers." EPS growth could be offset by share count increase.
Insight 4: Agentic AI = CPU TAM Expansion, Not GPU Replacement
Server CPU TAM $35B→$120-170B (2030). CPU:GPU ratio shifts from 1:4-1:8 to 1:1-1:2. This is additive, not substitutive. AMD is the only company selling both competitive CPUs (EPYC) and GPUs (Instinct). NVIDIA has no open-market CPU (Grace is captive). Intel has no AI GPU.
Insight 5: NVIDIA's 16x P/E = "Dominance Discount"
NVIDIA trades at 16x Fwd P/E, below the S&P 500 average (20x). The market prices NVIDIA's 90% share, 75% margins, and $1T revenue path as unsustainable. But PEG is 0.60 — if AI buildout continues, this is not a value trap but an opportunity.
Insight 6: Intel's Collapse = AMD's Pricing Power Window
AMD's ~70% ASP premium and 46.2% revenue share stem not only from EPYC's technical superiority but from Intel's multi-year competitive gap. Diamond Rapids delayed to 2027. Intel 18A yield issues. But Lip-Bu Tan's turnaround or ARM's volume segment pressure could close this window.
Insight 7: Inference Inflection = AMD's Best Opportunity & Ceiling
Inference exceeds training for the first time in 2026 ($20.6B). Inference constitutes 80-90% of lifetime AI cost. AMD's memory-rich architecture is advantageous for inference. Meta running 100% Llama on MI300X proves this. But inference is lower-margin and more open to custom silicon. AMD's GPU margins may remain below NVIDIA's.
