2.6 Key Growth Drivers (Detailed)

1. AI Training (GPU-GPU Interconnect): - NVIDIA's NVLink 6 protocol defines 400G SerDes/lane. At these speeds, copper cables experience signal degradation over distances shorter than 1 meter. - Scale-up networks (high-density rack-internal GPU connections) require ~10 times more bandwidth per GPU compared to scale-out (LightCounting / IPEC webinar). - AI data centers require up to 10 times more optical fiber than traditional cloud facilities (Hyper Photonix / Corning).

2. AI Inference (AI-RPC — Rack-to-Rack): - In inference clusters, GPUs can sit idle because copper is insufficient. Optical interconnects can increase rack-to-rack bandwidth by 8x while reducing power consumption by 5x (Piris Labs). - NVIDIA's Quantum-X Photonics CPO switch promises 3.5x reduction in optical power consumption (IEEE / NVIDIA).

3. 5G Backhaul and Telecom: - 5G mmWave and mid-band deployments are increasing demand for high-speed optical transceivers for fronthaul/backhaul. - A portion of 800G/1.6T transceivers will also be directed toward telecom backbone renewal.

4. Autonomous Driving and LiDAR: - An important sub-segment of the general photonics market. LiDAR sensors, advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), and robotics use photonic laser and detector technologies.

5. Advanced Semiconductors and EUV Lithography: - ASML's EUV systems are indispensable for 3nm and below production. This directly affects both AI chip production (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel) and photonic integrated circuit (PIC) production.

4.1 InP Wafer Supply Crisis: A Structural Scarcity

In 2025, global InP substrate demand reached ~2.0–2.1 million wafers (2-inch equivalent). Current production capacity, however, is only 600,000–700,000 wafers. The supply gap exceeds 70% and this gap will not narrow in 2026: demand will rise to 2.6–3.0 million wafers while capacity remains at ~750,000 (Omdia, Yole, XKH SemiTech).

This scarcity exploded prices. The 2-inch InP substrate price rose from ~$800 in early 2025 to $2,300–$2,500 in 2026; $3,000+ was seen in urgent spot order. The 6-inch InP wafer price surged 250% to $5,000 after China's export restrictions (Reuters, Mining Weekly).

NVIDIA, anticipating this crisis, is trying to lock down the InP laser supply chain by investing a total of $4B in Lumentum and Coherent in March 2026. With $2B equity in each company plus multi-billion dollar purchase commitments, this investment is the adaptation of NVIDIA's same "preemptive supply lock" strategy used in the CoWoS shortage to photonics.

10.4 Copper-to-Optical Transition: Not "Mandatory" but "Gradual"

The boundary between copper and optical in AI data centers is defined by physical distance and data rate:

Connection Type Distance Technology Speed Status
Scale-Up (rack internal, GPU-GPU) <2m, <1m Copper (DAC) 100G-400G/lane Dominant until 2028
Scale-Out (rack-to-rack) >2m Optics transceiver 800G-1.6T Mandatory, now
AI-RPC (beyond cluster) Long distance Optics 800G+ Mandatory

Broadcom Hock Tan OFC 2026, NVIDIA NVLink 6, Bank of America

Technical facts: - At 200G data rates, copper cables are only effective over ~2 meters; beyond that signal attenuation rises sharply, electrical losses reach 22 dB. - NVIDIA NVLink 6 defines 400G SerDes/lane; at these speeds copper connections are limited to distances shorter than 1 meter. - 800G passive DACs are now only effective up to 3 meters.

However, Broadcom CEO Hock Tan's critical distinction at OFC 2026: Copper will remain the dominant option at least until 2028 for rack-internal ultra-short-distance connections, due to cost advantages and relatively low power consumption. Scale-out (between racks) requires optics; scale-up (inside rack, <2m) requires copper which is sufficient and economical until 2028. This distinction shows that CPO will enter "optics everywhere" but "optics for long distances and high-density areas."

Power consumption dimension: Optical network power consumption constitutes approximately 10% of total computing power in AI factories. NVIDIA's CPO-based switches (Quantum-X Photonics) promise 3.5x reduction in optical power consumption.

11.4 Technical Challenges: Thermal, Serviceability, Manufacturing

Three fundamental technical barriers slowing CPO commercialization:

1. Thermal Management (Biggest Bottleneck)

CPO places highly temperature-sensitive lasers in the same package with a high-power ASIC. Simulations show 150°C+ temperatures when 16 CPO modules operate in parallel. Lasers exhibit RIN (Relative Intensity Noise) and linewidth degradation as temperature rises — this degrades optical signal quality. Thermal cross-talk is a serious challenge from the switch die to the optical engines.

2. Serviceability (Critical Concern)

In traditional CPO architecture, since optical engines are soldered to the ASIC, a single dead optical engine or failed laser can disable the entire switch — meaning a multi-million dollar loss. Solution: ELSFP (External Laser Source) and PLS (Pluggable Laser Source) approaches — separating lasers into external, replaceable modules to make field service possible. Both NVIDIA and Broadcom are adopting this solution.

3. Manufacturing Yield and Test

CPO requires multi-physics coupling of electrical, optical, thermal, and mechanical domains. 500 nanometer precision is required for fiber coupling. 2.5D/3D packaging (TSMC COUPE, FOWLP, hybrid bonding) is critical. BESI (BE Semiconductor Industries) has delivered hybrid bonding equipment for CPO assembly; according to Bank of America, CPO shows the highest growth potential in BESI's equipment volume guidance.

12.5 China Export Control and Indium Dominance

February 2025: InP Export Control

China added indium phosphide (InP) substrates to the export control list on February 4, 2025. These restrictions slow InP substrate exports and raise prices. According to AXT Inc's 2026 10-K report: "InP export permits represent the most significant challenge we currently face."

Indium Dominance

China supplies approximately ~70% of global indium production (US Geological Survey, 2024). Indium is the base raw material for InP production; this dominance creates a "material chokepoint" in the InP supply chain.

Chinese Producers

Yunnan Germanium aims to reach annual 450,000 InP wafer capacity with a 189 million yuan ($28M) investment. Local producers such as Guangdong Xiandao and Zhuhai Dingtai are expanding capacity — but their production will primarily remain for the domestic market.

AXT China Subsidiary

AXT's Chinese subsidiary Beijing Tongmei was only able to obtain first export permits in late June 2025. There is a large order backlog but permit delays constrain production.
