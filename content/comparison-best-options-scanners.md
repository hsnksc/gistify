## How We Evaluated These Scanners

We did not just read marketing pages. We opened trial accounts, ran identical screens across platforms, and scored each tool on six criteria that actually matter to active options traders:

1. **Scanner Speed & Coverage** — How many tickers does it cover? Does it update in real time, or is data delayed? Can you screen the entire options chain, or just the underlying?

2. **Options-Specific Filters** — Does it know what IV Rank is? Can you filter by delta, theta, or put/call ratio? Or is it just a stock screener with an options tab slapped on?

3. **Earnings Integration** — Earnings are the biggest catalyst in options trading. Does the scanner tell you *when* a company reports, what the expected move is, and whether IV is elevated?

4. **Risk Visualization** — Finding trades is easy. Understanding what you are risking is hard. Does the tool show you max loss, breakeven, or a risk matrix before you commit?

5. **Pricing & Value** — Is the free tier usable? Does the paid tier justify the price for an active trader?

6. **Workflow Fit** — Does it force you to export data to Excel, or can you actually make decisions inside the platform?

Every tool below was scored on these criteria. Let us dive into the full reviews.

## Finviz Review

**Website:** finviz.com | **Price:** Free tier + Elite at $24.95/month | **Best For:** Visual traders who want heatmaps and broad stock screening

Finviz is a legend in the retail trading world. It has been around since 2007, and for many traders, it was their first screener. The free version lets you filter over 8,000 stocks by market cap, sector, technical patterns, and even candlestick formations. The Elite tier adds real-time data, advanced charts, and backtesting.

But here is the catch: Finviz is fundamentally a *stock* screener. Its options data is limited to basic options chains and volume stats. You cannot screen by IV Rank, expected move, or Greeks. You cannot build a strategy and see a profit graph. You cannot see earnings context next to a setup.

**Pros:**
- Extremely fast visual screening with heatmaps and bubble charts
- Free tier is genuinely useful for broad market scanning
- Excellent for spotting sector rotation and momentum clusters
- Elite tier is affordable at under $25/month
- Backtesting and correlation tools are solid

**Cons:**
- Options-specific filters are almost nonexistent
- No IV Rank, no expected move, no Greeks
- Earnings data is a date only — no options context
- No strategy builder or risk visualization
- Free tier is delayed and has limited export capabilities

**Best For:** Traders who want a quick visual overview of the market before deciding where to dig deeper. If you already have an options analysis tool and just need a fast stock screener, Finviz Elite is a great add-on. If you want an all-in-one options scanner, this is not it.

**Verdict:** Finviz remains one of the best *stock* screeners on the market. As an options scanner, it is a starter tool. It tells you *where* to look, but not *what* to trade. **Gistify Score: 7.8/10**

## OptionStrat Review

**Website:** optionstrat.com | **Price:** Free tier + paid plans | **Best For:** Traders who build, compare, and backtest options strategies before entering

OptionStrat is different from every other tool on this list. It is not a scanner in the traditional sense — it is a *strategy builder*. You pick a stock, build a strategy (iron condor, calendar spread, straddle, etc.), and OptionStrat shows you a beautiful profit/loss graph, breakeven points, max risk/reward, and even how the Greeks shift over time.

The free tier is surprisingly generous. You can build unlimited strategies, view P/L graphs, and analyze historical performance. The paid tier adds features like custom alerts, probability of profit calculations, and more advanced backtesting.

Where OptionStrat falls short is *discovery*. It does not scan the market and tell you "AAPL has a straddle setup with a 4.2% expected move." You have to bring the idea to OptionStrat and then analyze it. That is a different workflow — powerful, but not a replacement for a scanner.

**Pros:**
- Best-in-class strategy builder and P/L visualization
- Free tier is genuinely usable for most traders
- Excellent for learning — seeing how spreads behave is invaluable
- Backtesting and historical performance analysis are solid
- Clean, intuitive interface

**Cons:**
- Not a market scanner — you bring your own ideas
- No real-time options flow or unusual volume alerts
- No earnings integration beyond basic dates
- No IV Rank screening or expected move data
- Can feel like a separate tool you need to pair with a screener

**Best For:** Traders who already have a watchlist and want to deeply analyze every trade before committing capital. If you sell spreads, trade iron condors, or teach options, OptionStrat is essential. If you need help *finding* trades, you will need another tool alongside it.

**Verdict:** OptionStrat is the best strategy analysis tool on the market. As a scanner, it does not compete. But every serious options trader should have it in their arsenal. **Gistify Score: 8.2/10**

## Unusual Whales Review

**Website:** unusualwhales.com | **Price:** $35–$99/month | **Best For:** Flow traders who follow institutional money, dark pool, and unusual options activity

Unusual Whales built its brand on one thing: showing you where the *big money* is going. It tracks unusual options flow, dark pool prints, and institutional-grade activity in real time. If someone just bought $2 million worth of out-of-the-money calls on a semiconductor stock, Unusual Whales will surface it within seconds.

The platform also offers a powerful screener that lets you filter by flow type, premium size, sector, and even politician trading activity. The dark pool data is genuinely useful for understanding hidden support and resistance levels.

The downside? Unusual Whales is a *flow* tool first, a *scanner* second. It does not help you calculate expected move, manage risk, or build strategies. It tells you *what* is happening, but not *how* to trade it. If you follow flow blindly without understanding the setup, you can get burned quickly.

**Pros:**
- Real-time unusual options flow is unmatched
- Dark pool data provides institutional context
- Powerful filters for flow size, sector, and sentiment
- Politician trading tracker is a unique feature
- Great for momentum and catalyst trading

**Cons:**
- Expensive — starts at $35/month and climbs fast
- No free tier
- No risk visualization or strategy builder
- Earnings data is minimal
- Can create FOMO — following flow without a plan is dangerous

**Best For:** Traders who treat flow as a confirmation signal, not a primary strategy. If you already have a system and want to see whether institutions are aligning with your thesis, Unusual Whales is excellent. If you need help constructing trades or managing risk, look elsewhere.

**Verdict:** Unusual Whales is the best flow tool on the market. As a comprehensive options scanner, it is narrowly focused. Use it as a confirmation layer, not a decision engine. **Gistify Score: 8.5/10**

## QuantWheel Review

**Website:** quantwheel.com | **Price:** Free sandbox + $27–$47/month | **Best For:** Premium sellers running the wheel, cash-secured puts, and covered calls

QuantWheel is built for one specific audience: options sellers. The screener is designed around the wheel strategy — cash-secured puts, covered calls, and rolling mechanics. It shows you annualized return, breakeven, and even how many contracts you need to sell to reach a target income goal.

The free sandbox is generous. You can build a watchlist, run basic screens, and see what the paid tier would surface. The paid tiers add more tickers, advanced filters, and portfolio tracking.

Where QuantWheel struggles is outside its niche. If you trade directional options, momentum plays, or complex multi-leg strategies, the tool feels limited. It is a scalpel, not a Swiss Army knife.

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for premium sellers
- Free sandbox is genuinely useful
- Income-targeting tools are unique
- Clean, focused interface
- Affordable compared to competitors

**Cons:**
- Narrow focus — not for directional traders
- No flow data or momentum scanning
- Limited earnings integration
- No risk matrix or scenario analysis
- Fewer tickers covered than broad market screeners

**Best For:** Traders who primarily sell premium and want a tool that thinks like they do. If you run the wheel on 10-20 stocks and care about annualized yield more than directional moves, QuantWheel is excellent. If you need a scanner for all strategy types, you will outgrow it.

**Verdict:** QuantWheel is the best tool for premium sellers. It does one thing exceptionally well. Just know that "one thing" is exactly what it does. **Gistify Score: 7.9/10**

## Which Scanner Should You Choose?

The right tool depends on your trading style, budget, and how many browser tabs you are willing to keep open.

**If you sell premium → QuantWheel or Gistify**
QuantWheel is purpose-built for wheel traders and cash-secured put sellers. Gistify adds earnings context and a risk matrix, which helps you avoid selling premium right before a volatile earnings report.

**If you follow flow → Unusual Whales**
There is no substitute for real-time unusual options flow. If your strategy revolves around following institutional money and dark pool prints, Unusual Whales is worth the price. Just remember: flow is a confirmation tool, not a strategy.

**If you build strategies → OptionStrat**
Every options trader should have OptionStrat. Even if you use another scanner to find trades, OptionStrat is where you analyze them. The P/L graphs and backtesting are unmatched.

**If you want everything in one place → Gistify**

Both offer genuinely usable free tiers. Finviz is better for visual scanning and heatmaps. Barchart is better if you also trade futures or want real-time data in the paid tier. Neither is options-specialized, but both will get you started without spending a dollar.

**If you trade earnings systematically → Market Chameleon**
The historical earnings data and expected move analytics are worth the $99/month if you run a structured earnings program. For casual earnings traders, it is probably overkill.

## FAQ: Best Options Scanner (2025)

### What is the best free options scanner?
Finviz and Barchart both offer solid free tiers for stock screening with basic options data. OptionStrat offers the most generous free tier for strategy analysis. Gistify also offers a free tier with momentum scanning and earnings context. If you want real-time flow data, you will need to pay — Unusual Whales has no free option.

### Is IV Rank important for options scanning?
Yes. IV Rank tells you whether a stock's current implied volatility is high or low relative to its recent history. Selling premium when IV Rank is high (above 50) and buying options when IV Rank is low (below 30) is a core edge in options trading. Any scanner that does not show IV Rank is leaving money on the table.

### Can I use multiple scanners together?
Absolutely. Many professional traders use Finviz for broad market screening, Unusual Whales for flow confirmation, OptionStrat for strategy analysis, and Gistify for earnings context and risk management. The question is whether you want to pay for and manage four subscriptions, or find one tool that covers 80% of your workflow.

### What is the difference between an options scanner and an options screener?
The terms are often used interchangeably, but technically a "scanner" continuously monitors the market and alerts you to new opportunities in real time, while a "screener" requires you to run a filter and returns a static list. Unusual Whales and Gistify lean toward scanner behavior. Finviz and Barchart are more traditional screeners.

### Should beginners use a paid options scanner?
Not necessarily. If you are new to options, start with free tools like Finviz, OptionStrat, and Gistify's free tier. Learn the basics of IV, delta, and expected move before paying for premium data. Once you are consistently trading and need real-time alerts or advanced analytics, a paid scanner becomes worth the investment.

### What makes Gistify different from other options scanners?
Gistify is designed as a unified workspace rather than a standalone scanner. It combines momentum scanning, earnings intelligence (IV Rank, expected move, setup suggestions), and a risk matrix in one dark-themed interface. The trade-off is that individual features may not be as deep as specialized competitors, but the workflow integration is unique.
