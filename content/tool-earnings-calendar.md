# Free Earnings Calendar (2025) — Upcoming Earnings with Options Context

**URL:** `/tools/earnings-calendar` | **Target Keyword:** earnings calendar 2025

Earnings season is where options traders make or break their quarter. A single earnings report can move a stock 10% overnight. The options market prices in that expected move through elevated implied volatility. But knowing *when* a company reports is only half the battle. You also need to know *how much* the market expects it to move, *whether* IV is already inflated, and *what strategy* gives you the best risk/reward.

This free earnings calendar goes beyond dates. It integrates IV Rank, expected move, and suggested setup types — the same context Gistify traders use to make informed decisions.

## How to Read This Calendar

Each row in the table below contains the data you need to evaluate an earnings play before the market opens:

- **Ticker & Company** — The stock symbol and company name
- **Date & Time** — The report date and whether it is Before Market Open (BMO) or After Market Close (AMC)
- **EPS Est** — The consensus earnings per share estimate (context for reaction magnitude)
- **IV Rank** — Implied Volatility Rank on a 0-100 scale. High = expensive options. Low = cheap options.
- **Exp. Move** — The expected price move derived from at-the-money straddle pricing. This is what the market is pricing in.
- **Setup** — The strategy type that best fits the risk/reward profile based on IV Rank and expected move

**How to use it:** Look for setups where the expected move is large enough to justify the risk, but IV Rank is not so high that post-earnings IV crush will erase your gains. The sweet spot is often IV Rank between 40-70 with an expected move of 4% or more.

## How to Use Earnings Data for Options Trading

Earnings reports are the most predictable catalysts in the market. You know exactly when they happen. The challenge is structuring a trade that profits from the expected move while protecting against the risks you cannot control.

**Step 1: Identify the expected move.**
The expected move column tells you what the options market is pricing in. If the expected move is ±5% and the stock has historically moved 8% on earnings, there might be a disconnect you can exploit. If the expected move is larger than historical average, the market may be overpricing risk.

**Step 2: Check IV Rank before entering.**
High IV Rank (above 70) means you are paying a premium for optionality. If you buy a straddle at IV Rank 85, you need the stock to move *more* than expected just to break even after IV crush. If IV Rank is moderate (40-70), the risk/reward is more balanced.

**Step 3: Choose the right setup.**
- **Straddle** — Buy a call and put at the same strike. Best when you expect a big move but do not know the direction. Works best with moderate IV Rank.
- **Strangle** — Buy a call and put at different strikes. Cheaper than a straddle but needs a larger move to profit. Good for high-volatility stocks.
- **Iron Condor** — Sell a call spread and a put spread. Profit if the stock stays within the expected move. Best when IV Rank is high and you believe the market is overpricing the move.
- **Spread** — Directional call or put spread. Best when you have a strong directional bias and want to limit risk.

**Step 4: Manage risk with position sizing.**
Never risk more than 2-5% of your account on a single earnings play. Even with perfect analysis, earnings are binary. A stock can beat estimates, raise guidance, and still sell off because the whisper number was even higher. Size accordingly.

## Why Options Traders Need More Than a Date

A standard earnings calendar tells you when a company reports. That is table stakes. But options traders need more:

- **Is implied volatility already inflated?** If IV Rank is 85, the market has already priced in a big move. Your edge is smaller.
- **What is the expected move?** Without this, you are guessing how far the stock might go.
- **What setup fits best?** A straddle is not always the right play. Sometimes an iron condor is better. Sometimes a simple directional spread is best.

## Pro Tips for Earnings Season

1. **Avoid the week before expiration.** 0DTE options around earnings have extreme gamma risk. A small move against you can wipe out the entire position. Use weekly options with at least 7-14 days to expiration.

2. **Watch the guidance, not just the numbers.** A company can beat EPS and still crash if forward guidance is weak. The options market prices the *reaction*, not just the report.

3. **Use the expected move as a stop.** If you sell an iron condor with wings at the expected move, you are essentially saying "the market is wrong about how far this stock will go." That is a valid thesis, but know what you are betting against.

4. **Correlations matter.** If NVDA reports on the same day as AMD, their moves may be correlated. Diversify your earnings plays across sectors to avoid concentration risk.
